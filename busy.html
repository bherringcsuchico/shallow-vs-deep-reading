The Invention of the Printing Press

In the mid-15th century~1450s, Johannes Gutenberg of Mainz, Germany, transformed human communication with the invention of the movable type printing press. Prior to Gutenberg, books in Europe were copied by hand, often by monks in monasteries. This process was slow, expensive, and limited literacy to a tiny elite.
